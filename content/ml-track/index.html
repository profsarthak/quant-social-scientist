ML Track (separate)

The predictive culture’s toolkit — bridged from the main spine, not woven into it.

Machine learning is kept deliberately separate from the causal main track, and bridged to it only where it earns a place. The reason is the argument made in Predictive Modelling: ML is, by default, the predictive culture. Pretending its tools answer causal questions is one of the most common and costly confusions in applied work.

That said, the frontier of social science increasingly uses ML in service of causal goals — estimating treatment-effect heterogeneity, handling high-dimensional controls — and this track follows that bridge carefully.

TipLevel

undergrad → graduate. ISLR is the spine; the two social-science ML monographs supply the bridge to causality.

The foundations of predictive modelling — the bias–variance trade-off, cross-validation, regularization, trees/forests/boosting, and classification — live in Part IV. This track goes deeper and broader: the flexible supervised methods Part IV deferred, plus the unsupervised half of machine learning the main spine never needed. Supervised learning predicts a label Y from features X; unsupervised learning finds structure in X with no label at all.
