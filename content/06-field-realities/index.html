6 · Field & Client Realities

Where rigour meets the messy world of measurement, incentives, and people who need an answer by Friday.

Academic training tends to stop at the clean estimate. Real research — in the field, for a client, inside an organisation — begins there. This module is what separates someone who can run a model from someone who can be trusted with a question. It is the project’s distinctive layer, and the source library is unusually strong here.

TipLevel

applied / professional. Bryman for method, Muller and Reinhart for the critical conscience.

The critical conscience

Two sources give this module its edge. Muller’s The Tyranny of Metrics is the warning that measuring a thing changes behaviour around it — that the number you optimise can quietly become the goal, displacing what it was meant to track. Reinhart’s Statistics Done Wrong keeps the methods honest. Together they make the case that judgement — knowing when not to trust the number — is the final skill of the quantitative researcher.

Where this leads

This is the destination of the main track: a researcher who holds academic rigour and industry realities in the same hand.

## The critical conscience

Two sources give this module its edge. **Muller's *The Tyranny of Metrics*** is the warning
that measuring a thing changes behaviour around it — that the number you optimise can quietly
become the goal, displacing what it was meant to track. **Reinhart's *Statistics Done
Wrong*** keeps the methods honest. Together they make the case that *judgement* — knowing
when not to trust the number — is the final skill of the quantitative researcher.
