III · Statistical Modelling: Regression

The regression workhorse — fitting lines, and knowing what they mean.

This is where statistics meets the social world’s questions. The centre of gravity is regression: a tool for holding things constant, summarising relationships, and — with care — estimating effects. The undergraduate core: OLS, its assumptions, its inference, its honest diagnostics, and the limited-dependent-variable models for outcomes that aren’t continuous.

TipLevel

undergrad → applied. Assumes Inference. Wooldridge is the spine; Mastering ’Metrics supplies the intuition.

The bridge to causality

Mastering ’Metrics frames regression as a way of making “other things equal.” That framing is the hinge between description and Causal Inference: a coefficient is only an effect if you have an argument for why the comparison is fair.

Where this leads

Onward to Part IV · Predictive Modelling — the computational turn, where the goal shifts from explaining to predicting, and the same regression machinery is judged by a completely different standard.

## The bridge to causality

*Mastering 'Metrics* frames regression as a way of making "other things equal." That framing
is the hinge between description and **[Causal Inference](../05-causal-inference/index.qmd)**:
a coefficient is only an *effect* if you have an argument for why the comparison is fair.
