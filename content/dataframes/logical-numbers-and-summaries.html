Logical Vectors, Numbers & Summaries

The small operations that power the verbs: how to ask yes/no questions of a column, transform numbers, and collapse them into the summaries that answer your question.

The dplyr verbs are only as good as the expressions you feed them. filter() needs a logical test; mutate() needs a numeric transformation; summarize() needs a summary. This entry is the toolbox of those small operations — comparisons and boolean logic, numeric helpers, and aggregation functions — plus the two traps that bite everyone: floating-point equality and the & / && distinction.

Logical vectors

A logical vector holds only TRUE, FALSE, or NA. (Wickham et al. 2023) You make one with the comparison operators — >, >=, <, <=, == (equal), != (not equal) — and combine logicals with boolean algebra: & (and), | (or), ! (not). (Wickham et al. 2023) A handy shorthand for a chain of “or”s is x %in% y, which is TRUE wherever x is one of the values in y.

WarningThe floating-point trap

Never use == on numbers with decimals. Computers use finite precision, so “every number you see is an approximation” — and sqrt(2)^2 == 2 actually evaluates to FALSE. (Wickham et al. 2023) Use dplyr::near(), which ignores tiny differences. This bug is silent and ruins filters and joins.

Note& vs && — vectorised vs short-circuit

Use & and | for vectorised logic — element by element, the kind filter() needs. Use && and || only for a single TRUE/FALSE in conditional execution (an if statement); they short-circuit, stopping the moment the answer is settled (&& quits at the first FALSE). (Wickham et al. 2023) Putting && inside filter() is a classic mistake.

For conditional values, if_else() handles a two-way choice (a test, a “yes” value, a “no” value), and case_when() handles multi-way choices without a tangle of nested ifs: (Wickham et al. 2023)

flights |>
  mutate(status = case_when(
    arr_delay < 0  ~ "early",
    arr_delay == 0 ~ "on time",
    arr_delay > 0  ~ "late",
    .default       = "unknown"        # catches NA and anything unmatched
  ))

Logicals are numbers in disguise

The most useful trick: in a numeric context FALSE becomes 0 and TRUE becomes 1. (Wickham et al. 2023) So inside summarize(), sum() of a logical counts the TRUEs and mean() of a logical is the proportion of TRUEs — no extra recoding:

flights |>
  group_by(dest) |>
  summarize(
    n_late    = sum(arr_delay > 0, na.rm = TRUE),   # count of late flights
    prop_late = mean(arr_delay > 0, na.rm = TRUE)   # share that were late
  )

Numeric transformations

The numeric helpers that show up constantly: (Wickham et al. 2023)

  • Modular arithmetic — %/% (integer division) and %% (remainder) split an integer apart (turn 1517 into 15 hours and 17 minutes).
  • Offsets — lead() and lag() refer to the next/previous value, essential for running differences and change-over-time.
  • Ranking — min_rank() (ties share a rank: 1, 2, 2, 4), with desc() to rank from the top; cousins row_number(), dense_rank(), percent_rank().
  • Binning — cut() “discretizes continuous variables” into labelled groups (turn income into brackets).
  • Cumulative aggregates — cumsum(), cumprod(), cummin(), cummax(), and dplyr’s cummean().

Summary functions

Summaries collapse a column to one number per group: (Wickham et al. 2023)

  • Counts: n() (group size), n_distinct(x) (number of unique values).
  • Location & spread: median(), IQR() (interquartile range), min(), max() — the robust pair when data is skewed.

These are the building blocks that, with group_by(), implement split–apply–combine.

Common pitfalls

WarningWhere this goes wrong

  • == on decimals. Floating-point approximation makes exact equality unreliable; use near(). (Wickham et al. 2023)

  • &&/|| in vectorised code. They return a single value and short-circuit; inside filter()/mutate() use &/|. (Wickham et al. 2023)

  • Forgetting na.rm = TRUE. A single NA makes sum/mean/median return NA (next entry); decide how missing is handled before aggregating. (Wickham et al. 2023)

  • case_when() with no default. Unmatched rows become NA; add .default (or an explicit catch-all) unless you mean that. (Wickham et al. 2023)
