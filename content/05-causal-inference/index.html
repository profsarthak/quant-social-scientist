V · The Summit: Causal Inference

From ‘these move together’ to ‘this causes that’ — the peak of the craft.

The summit of the whole manual, and the centre of gravity of the source library. The question is deceptively simple — what is the effect of X on Y? — and answering it honestly requires a framework for imagining the world that didn’t happen, plus the design and computational tools built in every part before this one.

The ladder of causation

Judea Pearl gives the whole summit its organizing picture: the Ladder of Causation, a three-rung hierarchy of the questions you can ask, each strictly above the last. (Pearl and Mackenzie 2018)

  1. Association — seeing. “What if I see…?” Find regularities in data: a customer who buys toothpaste also buys floss. This is the rung of correlation, ordinary statistics, and “model-blind” machine learning — it can predict, but it cannot say why. (Pearl and Mackenzie 2018) Everything in Parts I–IV lives here.
  2. Intervention — doing. “What if I do…?” Predict the effect of an action that changes the world — raise the price, administer the drug. Pearl’s line: “seeing smoke tells us a totally different story about the likelihood of fire than making smoke.” (Pearl and Mackenzie 2018) This Part is the climb to rung 2.
  3. Counterfactuals — imagining. “What if I had done…?” and “Why?” Reason about the world that didn’t happen for a specific unit — comparing reality to a “fictitious and contradictory world.” This, Pearl argues, is what most distinguishes human reasoning from a pattern-matcher. (Pearl and Mackenzie 2018)

The rungs are a one-way gate: “you cannot answer a question on rung two… using only data collected from rung one.” (Pearl and Mackenzie 2018) No amount of correlation becomes causation on its own, because “data are profoundly dumb about causal relationships” — they need a push from a causal model (a DAG, a potential-outcomes assumption, or a design). That push is exactly what this Part supplies. Read the rest of the summit as the engineering of the climb from rung 1 to rungs 2 and 3.
