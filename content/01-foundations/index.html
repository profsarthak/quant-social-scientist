I · Foundations: Probability & Data

The grammar of uncertainty, and what data actually is.

The bedrock. Before inference, before regression, before causality, you need a feel for randomness and the objects we use to tame it — and a feel for real data, which is messier than any textbook’s. The aim is not rigour for its own sake but fluency: enough probability and data sense that later ideas feel inevitable rather than arbitrary.

TipLevel

basic → undergrad. No calculus assumed at the start; introduced gently where it pays off.
